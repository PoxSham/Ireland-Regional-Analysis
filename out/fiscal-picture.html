Ireland's Public Finances in 2024

Ireland recorded a fiscal surplus of €22.6bn in 2024. Total tax revenue reached €126bn. Corporation tax now accounts for 22.3% of all taxes — the top 10 multinationals pay 57% of all CT receipts, creating a significant concentration risk.

Data: IFAC Fiscal Assessment Report 2024; CSO Government Finance Statistics 2024

Corporation Tax concentration risk: The top 10 multinationals account for 57% of all CT receipts. 88% of CT is paid by foreign-owned MNCs. The OECD Pillar Two 15% global minimum tax may reduce Ireland's competitiveness advantage and reduce future CT windfalls. IFAC has repeatedly flagged this as a structural fiscal risk.

National Debt as % of GDP vs GNI* (2019–2024)

GNI* is Ireland's preferred modified national income measure — GDP is distorted by MNC accounting

Source: NTMA; CSO; IFAC

Geography: Ireland (national) · Year: 2019–2024

GDP-based headline flatters Ireland's fiscal position. GNI* provides a truer picture of underlying fiscal capacity.
The GNI* distinction matters: Ireland's debt-to-GDP ratio of 38.3% appears very low by European standards, but the debt-to-GNI* ratio of 67.1% is a more meaningful indicator of the actual debt burden. IFAC uses GNI* as its primary fiscal anchor.
